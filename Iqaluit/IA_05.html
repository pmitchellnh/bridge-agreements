Lebensohl Over 1NT Interference

  1. Scope

    1. This is used when we open or directly overcall 1NT and the opponents bid 2 of a suit.

  2. Double is for penalty

  3. With a higher-ranking 5-cd suit:

    1. Suit bid is weak
      • 1NT (2♣) 2♥
      • Weak hearts, to play
    2. Going thru Lebensohl is invitational:
      • 1NT (2♣) 2NT! (p), 3♣! (p) 3♥
      • Invitational hand with 5 hearts
    3. Jump suit bid is GF
      • 1NT (2♣) 3♥
      • Game forcing hand with 5 hearts.

  4. With a lower-ranking 5-cd suit:

    1. Going thru Lebensohl is weak:
      • 1NT (2♠) 2NT! (p), 3♣! (p) 3♥
      • Weak hearts. To play.
    2. Suit bid is GF
      • 1NT (2♠) 3♥
      • Game forcing with 5 hearts
    3. Also responder can pass the 3♣! relay if they want to play in clubs.

  5. Direct Deny 3NT

    1. Direct 3NT bid over the opponents overcall denies a stopper
    2. If necessary, opener then bids 4 of his longest minor to run from 3NT and a playable fit is found elsewhere
      • 1NT (2♣) 3NT!
      • Game forcing, denying a club stopper
    3. Going through Lebensohl shows a stopper:
      • 1NT (2♣) 2NT! (p), 3♣! (p) 3NT!
      • Guarantees a club stopper

  6. Direct Deny Stayman

    1. Direct cuebid over the opponents overcall is Stayman, but denies a stopper
      • 1NT (2♦) 3♦!
      • Stayman, denying a diamond stopper
    2. Going through Lebensohl shows a stopper
      • 1NT (2♦) 2NT! (p), 3♣! (p) 3♦!
      • Stayman, guarantees a diamond stopper.
    3. The only Stayman sequence where the stopper showing bids don't apply is over a 2♣ overcall, because there isn't room to show both.

  7. When they bid a 2-suited overcall

    1. Two suits known (example: Cappeletti 2♦)
      1. Direct Deny 3NT denies stoppers in either of their suits (but plenty of values elsewhere!)
      2. Going thru Lebensohl 2NT and then bidding one of their suits shows a stopper in that suit and denies a stopper in the other. 'Slow shows'. In this one situation, the bid is NOT a Stayman cuebid.
      3. Going thru Lebensohl 2NT and then bidding 3NT promises a stopper in BOTH of their suits.
    2. One suit known (example: Cappeletti 2♥).
      1. Treat this as an overcall in the one known suit.
      2. Ignore the unknown suit for Lebensohl purposes.
