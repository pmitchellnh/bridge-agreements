  1. Introduction and General Approach

    1. Introduction

      2. Notes on formatting and symbols used on this card:

      Suits are referred to by their first letter (S,H,D,C) or by their suit symbols: ♠ ♥ ♦ ♣
      'M' is an unspecified major (♠ or ♥)
      'm' is an unspecified minor (♦ or ♣)
      '&' is any denomination (including NT)
      '$' is any suit (excludes NT)
      'O' is the other major, for sequences that include 'M', so 1M (2NT) 3O is either:
      1♠ (2NT) 3♥ or
      1♥ (2NT) 3♠
      'o' is the other minor, for sequences that include 'm', so 1m - 1M, 1NT - 2o is either:
      1♣ - 1♥, 1NT - 2♦ or
      1♣ - 1♠, 1NT - 2♦ or
      1♦ - 1♥, 1NT - 2♣ or
      1♦ - 1♠, 1NT - 2♣

      '*' means Double
      '**' means Redouble

      "!" indicates an alerted bid, e.g.:

      1♠ - 3♣!
      "!!" indicates an announced bid, e.g.:
      1♠ - 1NT!!

      Bid sequences generally start with opener.
      Passes are included where relevant (e.g., third and fourth hand openings).
      Uncontested auctions are in the form [opener] - [responder], [opener] - [responder], with commas separating each round and opponents' passes being implicit, for example:

      1♠ - 2♥, 3♦ - 4♥ or
      p - 1♠, 2♦! - 2♥!
      In contested auctions, opponents' bids will be in parentheses; passes will either be implicit, where obvious, or denoted by a 'p', for example:
      1♠ (2♦) 3♦ (p), 4♠

    2. General Approach - 2/1 Game Force

      1. Over any suit opening at the one-level, a natural, non-jumping two-level suit response by an unpassed hand is forcing to game. This includes:
        • 1♠ - 2♣
        • 1♠ - 2♦
        • 1♠ - 2♥
        • 1♥ - 2♣
        • 1♥ - 2♦
        • 1♦ - 2♣ (with one exception, as follows)
        • 1♦ - 2♣, 2& - 3♣ promises 10+ HCP and 6 clubs; game force is now OFF
      2. When opponents interfere in a game-forcing auction, we either persist to game or they play doubled
        1. When the interfering bid immediately follows the responder's 2/1 bid, then opener has the following responsibilities:
          1. With a full opener and appropriate defense (at least two quick tricks), opener may pass or double
          2. With a "Rule of 20" opener, appropriate only for offense, opener rebids suit (single-suiter) or bids a second suit (two-suiter)

  2. When We Open

    1. Notrump Openings

      1. 1NT = 15-17 HCP
      2. Responses to 1NT by a passed or unpassed hand, with no interference
        1. 2♣ is Stayman
          1. With 4-4 in the majors, opener bids 2♥
          2. Over 2♦ response, Smolen is on with 5/4M or 6/4M and GF values.
          3. See also, Extended Stayman Sequences
        2. 2♦!! and 2♥!! are Jacoby transfers
          • See also, Jacoby/Texas Transfers, including Superaccepts
        3. 2♠! is a transfer to clubs, with post-accept
          • See Four Suit Transfers
          2NT! is a transfer to diamonds, with post-accept (also used for 5-5 minors, weak)
          • With flat invitational hand, go through Stayman first.
          • See Four Suit Transfers
        4. 3♣! = 5/5 minors invitational
        5. With 5/5 minors weak, bid 2NT (transfer to diamonds w/ postaccept) and pass any response
        6. 3♦! = 5/5 minors game-forcing
          1. 3♥ by opener = 4 clubs and a doubleton (setting clubs as trump)
          2. 3♠ by opener = 4 diamonds and a doubleton (setting diamonds as trump)
          3. 3NT is to play (no interest in minor suit game or slam)
          4. 4♣ or 4♦ implies less than 4-card trump support OR a flat hand
        7. 3♥! = 5/5 majors invitational
        8. 3♠! = 5/5 majors game-forcing
          1. With 5/5/ majors weak use Stayman and bid hearts over 2♦ (see Extended Stayman Sequences )
        9. 3NT is to play
        10. 4♣ is Gerber (asking for aces)
        11. 4♦!! and 4♥!! are Texas transfers
        12. 4♠! is quantitative (Jabbour) with at least 4-4 in the minors (slam interest)
        13. 4NT is quantitative (not Blackwood)
      3. Responses to 1NT, with interference
        1. Over a completely artifical 2♣ (no suit known, as in a Cappelletti 2♣):
          • Systems are on
          • Double is Stayman
        2. Over any natural or artificial 2-level suit bid, where at least one suit is known:
          • Lebensohl is on
          • 4♣ is Gerber
          • 4♦!! and 4♥!! are Texas transfers
        3. Over 2NT (minors)
          • No system currently defined
          • Bids are natural and forcing for one round
        4. Over a completely artificial double (no suit known, as in a D.O.N.T.):
          • Systems are on
          • Redouble is undefined
        5. Over a penalty double:
          • Meckwell Escapes are on
        6. When partner passes, double by the 1NT opener is for takeout in the balancing seat and penalty-oriented in direct seat.
          • 1NT (2♥) p (p), * is takeout.
          • 1NT (p) p (2♥), * is penalty-oriented.
      4. Enemy Interference over Jacoby transfer
        1. Over a double of our Jacoby transfer to 2M:
          • Pass! = 2-card support, with **! by responder forcing the completion of the transfer
          • 2M! = 3-card support
          • Superaccepts are on (see Jacoby/Texas Transfers)
        2. Over a suit bid after our Jacoby transfer,to 2M:
          • Pass! = 2-card support (rebid transfer at 3-level to complete)
          • Double! = 3-card support (rebid transfer at 3-level to complete)
          • 3M! = 4-card support (any strength/pattern)
      5. 2NT = 20-21 HCP
      6. Responses to 2NT by a passed or unpassed hand, with no interference
        1. 3♣! is Puppet Stayman
        2. 3♦!! and 3♥!! are Jacoby transfers
        3. 3♠ is Minor-Suit Stayman.
          • 3NT! = No 4-card minor
            • 4♣! = RKC3014 in clubs (presumably x=x=4=6)
            • 4♦! = RKC3014 in diamonds (presumably x=x=6=4)
            • 4NT = quantitative
          • 4♣! = 4-card clubs, or 5-card and minimum
            • suit bid = cue
            • 4NT = RKC3014 (standard for clubs)
            • 5♣ = to play
          • 4♦! = 4-card diamonds, or 5-card and minimum
            • suit bid = cue
            • 4NT = RKC1430 (standard for diamonds)
            • 5♦ = to play
          • 4♥! = 5-card clubs and maximum (slam interest)
            • same responses as to 4♣!
          • 4♠! = 5-card diamonds and maximum (slam interest)
            • same responses as to 4♦!
        4. 3NT is to play
        5. 4♣ is Gerber
        6. 4♦!! and 4♥!! are Texas transfers
        7. 4♠! is quantitative (Jabbour) with at least 4-4 in the minors (slam interest)
        8. 4NT is quantitative (not Blackwood)
      7. 3NT! = Gambling (First or second seat)
        1. 7-card running minor with no outside A or K.
        2. Partner leaves it in with A or K in 3 suits AND at least a singleton in the running minor.
        3. Otherwise partner bids 4♣ and opener corrects to 4♦ if necessary.
      8. 3NT! = Gambling (Third or fourth seat)
        1. 7-card running minor, may have as little as one suit stopped and another half-stopped.
        2. To play. Partner cannot pull.
        3. Over interference, any rebid by opener is to play.

    2. Major Openings

      1. Opening of one in a major promises at least five cards in the suit, in all seats
      2. Responses by an unpassed hand, with no interference ("Reverse Bergen" raises)
        1. 5 high card points and a 4-3-3-3 hand and 3-card support: Forcing 1NT!! (rebid 2M)
          • If opener rebids 2NT (18-19), responder must pass
        2. 0-5 support points and 3-card support or less: pass
          • 1♥ - 2♠ is a Weak Jump Shift (6+ spades and fewer than 6 HCP)
        3. 0-5 support points and 4-card support: 3M! (e.g., 1♠ - 3♠!)
        4. 0-9 support points and 5-card support: 4M
        5. 6-9 support points and 3-card support: 1M (e.g., 1♠ - 2♠)
        6. 6-9 support points and 4-card support or better: 3♦!
        7. 6-12 support points and no fit (2-card or less): 1$ or Forcing 1NT!!
          • 1♥ - 1♠ promises 5 spades, responder has not limited hand
          • 1♥ - 1NT!! does not deny 4 spades
        8. 10-12 support points and 3-card support: Forcing 1NT!!, followed by a jump in the major (e.g., 1♠ - 1NT!, 2♣ - 3♠)
        9. 10-12 support points and 4-card support or better: 3♣!
          1. 3♦ rebid by opener asks for shortness (singleton or void):
            • 3M = no shortness
            • 3O = unspecified void
              • 3NT = where?
                • 4m = void in bid minor
                • 4♥ = void in other major
            • 3NT = singleton in other major
            • 4m = singleton in bid minor
        10. 13-15 support points, 3-card support, 4-3-3-3 distribution: 3NT!
        11. 13+ support points, no fit (2-card or less): 2$ (2/1 game forcing)
        12. 13+ support points, 3-card support, not flat: 2$ (2/1 game forcing)
          • 1♥ - 1♠ promises 5 spades, responder has not limited hand
          • 1♠ - 2♥ promises 5 hearts
          • 1M - 2m only promises 4 cards in minor (could be 4-3 in majors; 4-2 in minors).
        13. 13+ support points, 4-card support or better, balanced/semi-balanced: 2NT! (Jacoby)
          1. if holding GOOD side 5-card suit (a suit you would freely overcall with), bid 4 of it!
          2. if any singleton or void, bid 3 of it; repeat this cuebid, if possible in the auction, to show a void
          3. else opener is balanced.
            • 4M! is minimum 12-14 HCP
            • 3NT! is 15-16 HCP
            • 3M! is 17-21 HCP.
        14. 13+ support points, 4-card support or better, unbalanced: Splinter! the singleton or void (e.g., 1♠ - 4♦!)
          • Note: The singleton can NOT be an Ace or King
          • Rebidding the splinter suit shows a void
      3. Responses by a passed hand, with no interference (2-way Reverse Drury)
        1. 2♣ is 3-card limit raise
          1. 2M shows sub-minimum opener
          2. 2♦ shows opening hand that would not accept the invitational raise
          3. Any other bid shows full opener with game interest
        2. 2♦ is 4-card limit raise
          1. 2M shows sub-minimum opener or no interest in game
          2. Any other bid shows full opener with game interest
        3. Bergen Raises are off
        4. Forcing 1NT!! is now semi-forcing
          • Opener can pass with a balanced minimum
      4. Responses with interference
        1. Over an interfering double, Manfield is on
          1. Redouble! = 8+ HCP and no big fit (i.e., 3-card support or less)
            • Alertable, as it could show fewer than 10 points
            • If 8 or 9, then must have 2-card support for opener.
          2. 1♠ (over 1♥) shows 4 spades (not common)
          3. 1NT! = relay to 2♣! by opener, followed by:
            • Pass! or 2♦! = weak minor, to play (6-card suit)
            • 2M! = 3-card limit raise
            • 2O! = 4-card limit raise with an unspecified void (with better than limit raise, splinter!)
              • 2NT! by opener asks where; if hearts is trump, 3♥! shows spade void.
            • Over an interfering bid (e.g., 1♠ (x) 1NT (2♥)
              • Any action by opener must presume that partner was relaying to the weak minor, and would thus have to show extraordinary strength and/or shape (e.g., 5-0-4-4).
              • Assuming opener passes, 3M! by responder shows the 3-card limit raise, and
              • Double! shows the 4-card raise with a void.
                • 2NT! (or 3NT!) by opener asks where; if hearts is trump, 3♥! (or 4♥!) shows spade void.
          4. 2♣! = 3-card support, 6-9 support points
          5. 2♦! = 4-card support 6-9 support points
          6. 2M! = 3-card support, weak (0-5 support points)
          7. 2O! = 4-card limit raise with an unspecified singleton (with better than limit raise, splinter!)
            • 2NT! by opener asks where; if hearts is trump, 3♥! shows spade void.
          8. 2NT! = at least 4-card support, limit raise or better, balanced
          9. 3♣ or 3♦ = fit-showing jump
            • This shows strong support AND a good side suit in the suit bid
          10. 3M! = 4-card support, weak and preemptive (0-5 support points)
          11. 3O!, 4♣, 4♦ = splinter bids
          12. 4M! = 5-card support, to play (0-9 support points)
        2. By an unpassed hand, over an interfering suit bid
          1. Double is negative (through 3♠)
          2. Cue bid of opponent's suit is limit raise or better (10+ support points with 4+ support)
          3. 2M is competitive
          4. 3M is preemptive
          5. New suit is forcing for one round
          6. 2NT is natural, showing 10-12 HCP probably with a doubleton in our opened major.
          7. Jump in any new suit is Splinter!
        3. By a passed hand, over an interfering suit bid
          1. Drury is on when available
            • Over 2♣ interference, 2♦ shows either 3- or 4-card limit raise
            • Over 2♦ or higher interference, Drury is off
          2. Else cue bid of opponent's suit is limit raise or better (10+ support points with 4+ support), but be careful here -- opener could be light
          3. Double is negative (through 3♠)
          4. 2M is competitive
          5. 3M is preemptive
          6. New suit is non-forcing
          7. 2NT is natural, showing 10-11 HCP probably with a doubleton in our opened major. Be careful here!
        4. By an unpassed hand, over an interfering 1NT
          1. Double shows balance of power (8+ HCP)
            • We play, or they play doubled
          2. New suit is natural and non-forcing
          3. 2NT! is a game-forcing 2-suited (highly distributional) takeout
            • Partner bids tolerable suits up the line
        5. By a passed hand, over an interfering 1NT
          1. Drury is on
          2. Double shows balance of power (10+ HCP) (careful, opener could be light!!)
            • We play, or they play doubled
          3. New suit is natural and non-forcing
        6. By a passed or unpassed hand, over an interfering cue bid or unusual 2NT
          1. See Defense Vs Two-Suited Overcalls

    3. Minor Openings

      1. Open better minor, promising at least 3-cards
      2. Responses by an unpassed or passed hand, with no interference (Inverted Minors with Criss-Cross)
        1. 1♦ (over 1♣) strongly suggests lack of 4-card major
          • If later bidding suggests a 4-card major, then count on the diamonds to be a very good suit (source of tricks)
        2. 1M = 4-card major. Bid 4-card major rather than NT or inverted minor, and also frequently bypass 4+ diamonds over a 1C opener.
        3. 1NT = 6-10 HCP; denies a 4-card major
        4. 2♣ (over 1♦) denies a 4-card major and:
          1. is game forcing unless the responder rebids 3♣ next time around
          2. a rebid of 3♣ shows 6+ clubs and 10+ HCP; game force is OFF
        5. 2m! = game-forcing points with 5-card support; denies a 4-card major.
        6. 2♦! (over 1♣) = limit raise in clubs (Criss-Cross); denies a 4-card major
          1. Opener rebids 3♣ to reject limit raise
        7. 2M! = weak jump shift (6-card major, 0-5 points)
        8. 2NT = 11-12 HCP; denies a 4-card major
        9. 3m! = weak and preemptive (5-9 support points with 5-card support); denies a 4-card major.
        10. 3♣! (over 1♦) = limit raise in diamonds (Criss-Cross); denies a 4-card major
          1. Opener rebids 3♦ to reject limit raise
        11. 3NT = 13-15 HCP; denies a 4-card major
      3. Continuation after a game-forcing single raise (1m - 2m!)
        1. Opener has a singleton or void
          1. Opener is real minimum - say 12-14. Just rebid our minor at the 3 level.
          2. Opener is at least medium - say 15+. Now opener splinters into the suit which has a singleton or void. Example: 1D - 2D, 3H! shows a heart singleton or void in opener's hand.
        2. Opener has no singleton or void. The partnership now searches for a possible notrump game and reverts to the minor if it is not possible.
          1. Opener bids 2NT with a minimum (12-14 HCP) and both majors stopped.
          2. Opener bids 3NT with 15+ and both majors stopped.
          3. With only 1 major stopped, opener bids the stopped major!.
            1. With the other major and the other minor stopped, responder bids 2 or 3 NT depending on strength.
            2. With only 1 or the other, responder bids it!.
              • In this case a followup NT bid by opener shows a 1/2 stopper in the 4th suit which should be pulled if responder does not also have 1/2 stopper in the 4th suit.
          4. With neither major stopped but with a stopper in the other minor, opener bids the other minor. Responder needs to stop both majors to bid 3NT else reverts to original minor.
          5. With neither major stopped and no stopper in the other minor, opener rebids original minor.
      4. Responses by a passed or unpassed hand, with interference
        1. Over an interfering double (Flip-flop)
          1. Pass = typically less than 8 points
          2. Redouble shows 10+ points with no big fit (less than 5-card support)
          3. 1M tends to show a 5-card suit (remember, RHO just doubled, ostensibly for the majors)
          4. 1NT = 8-10 high-card points, balanced
          5. 2m = competitive: 6-10 high-card points, 4-card support
          6. 2NT! = preemptive: 5-9 support points, 5-card support (Flip-flop)
            • Opener typically "pulls" to 3m
          7. 3m! = 10+ support-points, 5-card support (Flip-flop)
            • Opener can now "right-side" a 3NT bid, if appropriate
        2. Over an interfering suit bid
          1. Double is negative (through 3♠)
          2. Cue bid of opponent's suit is limit raise or better (10+ support points with 5+ support)
          3. 1NT = 8-10 high-card points, balanced; generally at least a stopper in RHO's suit
          4. 2m = competitive: 6-10 high-card points, 4-card support
          5. 3m = preemptive: 5-9 support points, 5-card support
          6. New suit is forcing for one round
          7. 2NT = natural, 10-12 HCP; should have at least a stopper in RHO's suit (perhaps two)
          8. 3NT = natural and to play; better have no concerns about RHO's suit
        3. Over an interfering 1NT
          1. Double shows balance of power (8+ HCP)
            • We play, or they play doubled
          2. New suit is natural and non-forcing
          3. 2NT! is a game-forcing 2-suited (highly distributional) takeout
            • Partner bids tolerable suits up the line
        4. Over an interfering cue bid or unusual 2NT
          1. See Defense Vs Two-Suited Overcalls

    4. Two Club Opening: Strong, Artificial and Forcing

      1. Showing 22+ HCP or equivalent playing strength (4-loser hand)
      2. Forcing to game with one exception:
        1. 2♣ - 2♥!, 2NT (can be passed)
      3. Response by a passed or unpassed hand, with no interference
        1. 2♦! = semi-positive: at least a King or two Queens
        2. 2♥! = super-negative: no Kings, and at most one Queen
        3. 2♠! = positive: 5-card heart suit with at least two of the top three honors
        4. 2NT! = positive: 5-card spade suit with at least two of the top three honors
      4. Response by a passed or unpassed hand, with interference
        1. Over an interfering double
          1. Redouble! = super-negative
          2. Pass! = semi-positive
          3. 2♦! = positive for diamonds
          4. 2♥! = positive for hearts
          5. 2♠! = positive for spades
        2. Over an interfering 2-level suit bid
          1. Double! = super-negative
          2. Pass! = semi-positive
          3. New suit! = positive in suit bid
          4. Cue bid of opponent's overcall! = semi-positive 4-4-4-1, with singleton in opponent's suit
        3. Over any interfering bid of 2NT or higher
          1. Pass! = forcing
      5. NT rebids by opener
        1. 2NT = 22-24 HCP
          1. All 2NT systems are on, including Puppet Stayman, transfers, Gerber, Jabbour, etc.
        2. 3NT = 25-27 HCP
          1. 4♣ = Stayman
          2. 4♦!!/4♥!! = transfers
          3. 4♠! = Jabbour
          4. 4NT = Quantitative
          5. 5♣! = "SuperGerber"
            • 5♦ = 0 or 4
            • 5♥ = 1
            • 5♠ = 2
            • 5NT = 3
      6. Over suit rebids by opener
        1. 2NT = no big fit
        2. New suit, no jump = natural

    5. Two Diamond Opening: Flannery

      1. 11-15 HCP, 4 spades and 5 or 6 hearts.
      2. Responses by a passed or unpassed hand, with no interference
        1. 2♥/2♠ = to play
        2. 2NT! = describe your minor holding
          1. 3♣! I have 3 clubs and 1 diamond
          2. 3♦! I have 3 diamonds and 1 club
          3. 4♣! I have 4 clubs and no diamonds
          4. 4♦! I have 4 diamonds and no clubs
          5. 3♥! I have 2 clubs, 2 diamonds and minimum (11-13 HCP)
          6. 3♠! I have 2 clubs, 2 diamonds and maximum (14-15 HCP)
          7. 3NT! I have 6 hearts
        3. 3♣ = to play
        4. 3♦! Relay to 3♥. Game is certain in a major and slam is possible. Responder wishes to establish trump.
          • Opener bids 3♥!
          • Responder bids 3♠! to correct, else starts cue bid sequence or RKC Blackwood
          • If responder bids 3♠!, opener cue bids, else bids 4♠
        5. 3♥/3♠ = invitational in hearts/spades
          • Opener passes with a minimum, 11-13
          • Opener bids game with a maxumum, 14-15
        6. 3NT = to play
        7. 4♥/4♠ = to play

    6. Two Heart and Two Spade Openings: Weak Two Bids

      1. 5-10 HCP
      2. 6-card suit with at least one of the top three honors
      3. No side 4-card major should be held if in 1st or 2nd seat
      4. Responses, with no interference
        1. 2NT = feature ask. Opener rebids:
          1. With a minimum (5-7), just rebid the suit, whether you have a feature or not.
          2. With a maximum (8+), show a feature (an outside ace or king).
          3. With a maximum (8+) and no feature, bid 3NT.
        2. New Suit is forcing.
          1. Opener raises new suit with 3-card support.
          2. Opener returns to own suit without 3-card support.
          3. If 2NT is a possible bid then it would show 2-card support.
            • Example: 2♥ - 2♠, 2NT! would show 2 card spade support.
          4. If new suit mentioned is at a higher level than 2NT, then 2-card support cannot be shown
            • Example: 2♥ - 3♣, 3♥ must be bid even with 2 clubs.
        3. 4♣! = Modified RKC Gerber
          1. 4♦! = Queen of trump, no keys
          2. 4♥! = No queen, one key
          3. 4♠! = Queen, one key
          4. 4NT = No queen, two keys
          5. 5♣! = Queen, two keys
      5. Responses, with interference
        1. Over an interfering double (McCabe)
          1. Redouble! = feature ask, as above.
          2. Suit bid = support for opener, but asks for suit bid to be led if opponents declare
          3. 2NT! = relay to 3♣!
            • Responder passes or corrects, to play
          4. 3M continues the preempt. This is NOT invitational.
          5. 4M is to play

    7. Preemptive Openings

      1. 3-level bids generally show 7 cards, light to very light.
      2. 4-level bids generally show 8 cards, light to very light.
      3. 3- and 4-level preemptive openers DO NOT meet the Rule of 20.
      4. Responses
        1. Raise is continue of preempt; not invitational
        2. New suit is forcing one round by a passed hand
          • Unless already at game level, e.g., 3♠ - 4♥
        3. New suit is forcing to game by an unpassed hand
        4. 3NT is to play
        5. 4♣ over 3♦, 3♥, or 3♠ = Modified RKC Gerber (see in Weak-Two section 4.c, above).
        6. 4♦ over 3♣ = Modified RKC Gerber, with responses pushed one level up

  3. When They Open

    1. Direct Doubles

      1. Takeout Double
        1. Takeout double hand is 12+ HCP and short in enemy suit (nothing more than weak doubleton).
        2. Example: over enemy 1H opening, double for takeout hands such as:
          • AQxx x KJxx Kxxx
          • AKxx x Axx Jxxxx
          • AKxx xx Qxxx AJx
      2. Responses to Takeout Double
        1. 0-8 HCP bid
        2. 9-12 HCP jump bid
        3. 13+ HCP cuebid opponent's suit
        4. Note that doubler will bid again with a strong double
      3. Strong Double
        1. 16+ HCP and a good suit and unable to overcall 1NT
        2. 16-19 HCP - Double and then bid your suit or NT after partner (who will assume takeout double) responds.
        3. 20+ HCP - Double and then cuebid enemy first suit.

    2. Simple Overcalls

      1. Direct Overcall: 7-17 HCP.
        • With 18-20, double first and then bid suit.
        • With 21+, double first and then cuebid enemy suit.
      2. Balancing.

    3. Jump Overcalls

      1. Direct: Weak
      2. Balancing: Strong!, 12+ HCP with a 1-suiter of at least 6 cards. Partner can raise or bid 2NT to invite game.

    4. Notrump Overcalls

      1. Direct. 15-18 HCP with systems ON, including Lebensohl!.
      2. Balancing. 10-14 HCP with systems OFF.
      3. Unusual (Jump). Overcall shows 5/5 in 2 lowest unbid suits and is WEAK (4-10) or STRONG (16+).
        • The STRONG hand is shown by taking another bid.
        • With intermediate hands, we just bid our suits.

    5. Over Opponents' Opening Preempt

      1. Takeout Doubles thru 4♥.
      2. Double of 4♠ or more is penalty.
      3. 4NT is a strong minor takeout over 4♥
      4. 4NT is any strong two-suiter over 4♠
        • Bid 3-card suits up the line to find a fit.
      5. Lebensohl! and other defense to weak-2's.

    6. Direct Cuebids

      1. Direct = Michaels
        1. We play this as weak (4-10 HCP) or strong (16+ HCP)
        2. With 11-15 HCP, bid the suits
        3. The strong is shown by taking another bid
        4. Responses must assume the weak hand
        5. Over (1m) 2m, showing 5/5 in the majors
          1. Non-jump bid in major is signoff.
          2. Jump in major is invitational to game.
          3. 4M is to play
          4. Bid other minor with 7+ card suit and no fit at all.
          5. Pass with 7+ of bid minor and no fit at all.
          6. Notrump raises should be avoided unless VERY strong (20+ HCP), no fit for either major and shape like 1255.
        6. Over (1M) 2M, showing 5/5 in the other major and an undisclosed minor
          1. Non-jump bid in our major is signoff.
          2. Jump in our major is invitational to game.
          3. 4 of our major is to play.
          4. Cheapest NT response! asks what the minor is
            • Michaels bidder should respond with the 5-card minor.
          5. Bid of a minor is to PLAY
            • (1♥) 2♥ (p) 3♣
            • This is long clubs to play, NOT offering a choice of minors.
      2. 4th seat and opponent has bid two suits = "Skewed cue"
        1. Cue bid lower ranking suit = 6/4, with 6 in lower of unbid suits
        2. Cue bid higher ranking suit = 6/4, with 6 in higher of unbid suits
          • e.g., (1♣) p (1♠) 2♣! = 6 diamonds and 4 hearts
          • e.g., (1♣) p (1♠) 2♠! = 6 hearts and 4 diamonds
        3. Note that 5/5 hands will use Unusual NT after 2 suits are bid
      3. Balancing = strong (12+) 5/5 2-suiter in ANY 2-suits
        • e.g., (1♦) p (p) 2♦!
        • Partner bids tolerable (3-card) suits up the line to find fit

    7. Defense vs No Trump

      1. In direct seat:
        1. D.O.N.T. against strong NT opener (high end 16+)
        2. Cappelletti against weak NT opener (high end 15-)
          1. Double is for penalty, showing values and defense
          2. 2♣ shows a single-suited hand
          3. 2♦ shows the majors
          4. 2♥ shows hearts and an undisclosed minor
          5. 2♠ shows spades and an undisclosed minor
          6. 2NT shows both minors
          7. 3-level bids are natural (define strength and/or length?)
      2. In balancing seat ( (1NT) p (p) ? )
        1. Double! shows a balanced 10+ with at least 4/4 in the majors
          • Partner may elect to leave the double in for penalty
          • Else partner bids best major, to play
        2. 2♣! shows an unbalanced 10+, with at least 4/4 in the majors
          • Partner bids best major, to play
        3. All other bids are natural
      3. After opponents have bid more than once ( (1NT) p (2♣) ? )
        1. Systems OFF
        2. All bids are natural

  4. Miscellaneous Agreements

    1. Over Opponents' Takeout Double

      1. With 0-5
        1. Pass unless you can WeakJumpShift!.
      2. With 6-9,
        1. raise partner with adequate trumps. a jump raise! is preemptive.
        2. bid 1NT with balanced hand (but can pass with 6-7 HCP)
        3. With 5-card suit, bid it.
      3. With 10+
        1. redouble for penalty, future NT or future raise of partner with 3-card M or 4-card m support.
        2. 2NT! = 4-card M or 5-card m support for partner. In this case 10 points means 10 support points rather than HCP.

    2. Special Doubles

      1. Negative Doubles thru 3♠
      2. Support Doubles and Redoubles thru 2M
        1. On when we open 1m (or 1♥) and partner responds 1M
          • 1♣ (1♦) 1♥ (2♦), ?
          • 1♦ (p) 1♠ (2♥), ?
        2. The one exception is when they bid our responder's major in 4th seat as NATURAL.
          • 1♣ (p) 1♥ (2♥!), ? -- where 2♥ is natural.
        3. A double! by opener now shows 3-card support for partner
        4. If RHO doubled, a redouble! by opener also shows 3-card support for partner
        5. A raise of partner's suit! by opener promises 4-card support
        6. A pass by opener shows a hand with less than 3-card support (not alertable)
        7. If RHO overcalls above 2 of our responder's major, then support doubles are OFF and a double would be for penalty.
        8. Note that even though 1♥ - 1♠ promises 5 spades, support doubles are still on over interference
      3. Responsive Doubles thru 2♠
        1. A responsive double is a response to partner's double or overcall when the opponents open and raise in the same suit.
        2. When partner doubles and RHO raises opener
          1. Double! shows at least 6 HCP and interest in locating a fit.
          2. If the enemy is bidding a minor suit, a responsive double asks partner to pick a major.
          3. If the opponents are bidding a major suit, a responsive double requests partner to choose a minor.
            • (1♠) * (2♠) *! = pick a minor
            • (1♦) * (2♦) *! = pick a major
        3. When partner has made a simple overcall in a suit AND RHO raises opener
          1. Double! shows at least 6 HCP support for the two unbid suits
            • (1♦) 1♠ (2♦) *! = pick hearts or clubs, no support for spades
        4. Responsive doubles are OFF if the enemy bids two suits, if partner makes a jump overcall, if enemy first bid is a preempt, or if partner overcalls in NT.

    3. Slam Conventions

      1. Gerber only:
        1. 1NT - 4♣
        2. 2NT - 4♣
        3. 2♣ - 2♦!, 2NT - 4♣
        4. (Weak 2) 2NT (p) 4♣
      2. Roman Keycard
        1. RKC3014 for clubs and RKC1430 for other suits. Note that the RKC trump suit is either the clearly agreed trump or (if unclear), the last natural suit bid by either partner.
        2. Queen-ask is cheapest nontrump suit over a 5♣ or a 5♦ response.
          1. NO = bid trump as cheaply as possible.
          2. YES = cheapest nontrump king if you have one
          3. YES = else jump in trump unless that means 7
          4. YES = else 5NT
        3. A 5NT bid following a response to 4NT is a request to show your cheapest nontrump specific king, if any.
          1. Without one, bid trump.
        4. Void-showing responses:
          • a useful void is a void in a suit other than partner's first bid suit.
          • a direct 5NT response to 4NT shows an EVEN number of keycards and a useful void.
          • a 6-level response to 4NT, below agreed trump suit shows a useful void in that specific suit and an ODD number of keycards.
          • a 6-level response in the agreed trump suit shows an ODD number of keycards and a useful void in some suit ranking ABOVE agreed trump suit.
        5. a 6-level cuebid after hearing a response to a RKC 4NT asks partner to bid a grand with 3rd round control of the suit cuebid.
        6. Note that all unusual RKC auctions (exclusion RKC, RKC Gerber, etc.) are 3014. 1430 is only used in standard ♦, ♥, and ♠ auctions.
      3. Slam Interference
        1. DOPI below 5 of our suit.
          1. Double = 0 or 3 (regardless of what flavor we were playing, 3014 or 1430)
          2. Pass = 1 or 4 (regardless of what flavor we were playing)
          3. Next level = 2 without queen, etc.
        2. DEPO at or over 5 of our suit.

    4. Miscellaneous Agreements

      1. Assorted Unusual Splinters

        1. A bid such as 1♥ - 1♠, 4♦! is a splinter by opener showing 4-card support, 19+ sps and a diamond void or singleton.
          • The singleton cannot be ace or king.
          • Rebidding the splinter suit shows a void!
          • Still ON over interference (example 1♥ (p) 1♠ (2♣), 4♣! (or 4♦!) is still a splinter).
        2. Singleton Splinters. All these auctions are singleton splinters by opener! Note that 1 level higher is therefore a void.
          • 1♣-1♠, 3♦!
          • 1♣-1♠, 3♥!
          • 1♣-1♥, 3♦!
          • 1♦-1♠, 3♥!
        3. Also 1♣-1♦, 1♥-3♠! is a splinter by responder in support of hearts.

      2. SOS Redoubles

        • A redouble with no other conventional meaning is for SOS. Partner is requested to bid - usually in order to avoid a bad penalty.

      3. Weak Jump Shifts

        1. Any jump shift to two of a major by responder when we open is a weak jump shift. It shows:
          1. Less than 6 HCP
          2. A 6-card suit
            • e.g., 1♦ - 2♠!)
        2. Over WJS, 2NT! is conventional and requests Ogust-like responses in context:
          • 3♣! = bad hand, bad suit
          • 3♦! = bad hand, good suit
          • 3♥! = good hand, bad suit
          • 3♠! = good hand, good suit
          • Note that "good" and "bad" must be taken in context, so a "good" hand is one with 4 or 5 HCP, and a "good" suit is one that is relatively solid given your points.

      4. New Minor Forcing!

        1. On in the following cases:
          1. 1m - 1M, 1NT - 2o!
          2. 1m - 1M, 2NT - 3o!
          3. 1m - 1M, 2m - 2o! or 3o!
          4. 1m - 1M, 3m - 3o! or 4o!
        2. Off over interference.
        3. Artificial and forcing for one round, showing:
          1. 5-cards in the responding major
          2. 10+ HCP over 1NT or 2m rebid
          3. Game-going values over 2NT or 3m rebid
        4. Opener's choices after NMF over 1NT
          1. With 3-card support bid 2M (12-13 HCP) or jump to 3M (14-15 HCP)
          2. With less than 3-card support, but stoppers in unbid suits, bid 2NT (12-13) or 3NT (14-15)
          3. With less than 3-card support, with one unbid suit stopped, bit the stopped suit
          4. Otherwise, wing it
            • e.g., 1♦ - 1♥, 1NT - 2♣!
            • Holding: xxx KQ AKxxx xxx, you'll have to choose between 2♦ and 2♥

      5. Fourth Suit Forcing!

        1. Artificial and forcing for one round, showing
          1. 10+ HCP
          2. Generally a 5-card major, but sometimes just no comfortable rebid
          3. Can be use as a temporizing bid to show extra strength with a subsequent bid
          4. Some people play this game-forcing; we prefer to consider it highly suggestive of game. FSF bidder can pass at any time, but opener should strive to keep the bidding alive if at all in doubt.
        2. Off over interference.
        3. Also off in the following cases:
          1. Bidding up the line (1♣ - 1♦, 1♥ - 1♠)
          2. When the fourth suit is a jump bid, showing extra strength and a two-suiter (e.g., 1♦ - 1♥, 1♠ - 3♣)
        4. Opener's choices
          1. With 3-card support for responder's first suit, bid it. Jump if game has play across from even a bare 10 HCP
          2. Bid 2NT or 3NT with stopper in fourth suit
          3. Rebid own with extra length
          4. Raise the artificial fourth suit with 4+ cards and no other bid

      6. Lebensohl Over Reverses!

        1. After a reverse is established (e.g., 1♣ - 1♥, 2♦):
        2. Responder bids anything except 2NT! = 8+ HCP
          1. Game force is ON.
        3. Responder bids 2NT! = less than 8 HCP; may want to bail shy of game
          1. Opener bids 3♣! = minimum reverse (16-18)
            1. Game force is OFF.
            2. Responder passes or makes a DROP DEAD final bid.
          2. Opener bid anything else! = maximum reverse (19-21)
            1. Game force is ON.

      7. Defense vs Their Flannery

        1. Double! = 15+ HCP NT distribution
        2. 2♥! = 3 suited takeout (all but hearts)
        3. 2♠ = spades
        4. 2NT! = minors
        5. 3m = natural

      8. Defense vs 1♣! or 1♦! Strong, Artificial and Forcing (ROSCOE!)

        1. Suit overcalls at the one level are natural
        2. 1NT shows a single-suited hand
        3. Two-level responses are same as D.O.N.T.
          1. 2♣! = clubs and a higher suit
          2. 2♦! = diamonds and a major
          3. 2♥! = both majors
          4. 2♠! = weak, 6-card spade suit (weaker than 1NT, then spades)
          5. 2NT! = both minors

      9. Western Cue and related bid

        1. Identified by a bid at the 3-level of their suit in a competitive auction.
          • e.g., 1♣ (1♥) 1♠ (2♥), 3♥ is western cue.
          • also, (1♦) 3♦ is a related bid.
        2. Both types ask partner to bid 3NT with a stopper in the cued suit.

      10. Defense vs Two-Suited Overcalls

        1. "Unusual over unusual"
        2. ONLY when we open a major

      11. Lightner Doubles vs. slams and 3NT.

        1. Double of slam by non-leader
          1. Does not apply to sacrifice slams
          2. Lead should be selected in this order:
            • A side suit bid by dummy or declarer
            • Leader's strongest suit
            • Never a suit bid by our side
          3. In general, the double announces that something strange is going on and time should be taken before leading to work out your best guess as to what that is
        2. Double of 3NT by non-leader
          1. Lead should be selected in this order:
            • Lead partner's suit, if he has bid
            • Lead your suit, if you have bid and partner has not
            • Else lead dummy's first bid suit
            • Else lead your shortest, weakest suit. In this case, the double says, "Partner I have a SOLID suit here; please find it!"

  5. Leads and Carding

    1. Opening Leads

      1. Leads are third- or fifth-best against both suits and no trump.
        • This is true even if the suit is garbage -- we don't lead MUD and we don't lead "top of nothing"
        • This means a low card does NOT promise an honor
        • Exception: When leading a suit bid by partner, top card may be appropriate even if unsupported
      2. Honor sequence leads are normal, except for Ace from AK (King is lead if AK is doubleton)
      3. Against no trump, the lead of an Ace or Queen requests partner to drop an honor or give count

    2. Defensive carding

      1. Upside down attitude and discards.
      2. Standard count and suit preference.
      3. Primary signal to partner's opening lead: Obvious Shift
      4. Primary signal to first lead of subsequent suits by partner: attitude
      5. Primary signal on first discard: attitude
      6. Show suit preference, as feasible, when playing to declarer's winners (e.g. drawing trump)
      7. Count signals used in the following situations:
        1. At the 6-level on the lead of a King
        2. Against NT on the lead of an Ace or Queen
        3. Helping partner to hold up an Ace or King
        4. When cashing out and the high cards are known
