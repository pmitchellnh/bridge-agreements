Negative Doubles

  1. Negative Doubles

    1. When used?
      1. This double is a tool of responder only and is never used by opener or overcallers.
      2. It is set up when partner opens and RHO overcalls in a suit at or below 3♠.
      3. A double by responder is now a negative double.
      4. A double of an opponent's overcall over 3♠ is penalty.
      5. A double of an opponent's 1NT or 2NT overcall is penalty.
    2. Strength (unlimited at high end)
      1. if at 1-level, shows 6+ HCP
      2. if at 2-level, shows 8+ HCP
      3. if at 3-level, shows 10+ HCP
    3. Shape
      1. Shows 4 cards in unbid major(s) and tolerance (3 cards) for unbid minors.
      2. Examples:
        • 1♣ (1♦) * = two 4-card majors and 6+ HCP
        • 1♣ (1♦) 1♥ = 4 hearts and less than 4 spades, because you would negative double with 4 of each.
        • 1♦ (1♥) * = 4 spades, 3+ clubs and 6+ HCP
        • 1♦ (1♥) 1♠ = 5 spades and 6+ HCP. Note that you must have 5 spades for this bid.
        • 1♦ (2♣) * = two 4-card majors and 8+ HCP
        • 1♥ (2♠) * = two 4-card minors (when no majors are indicated by the double, minors should be 4+) and 8+ HCP
    4. Frequently asked questions
      1. What to do with a trump stack in overcaller's suit?
        • Pass and wait for the reopening double (see section C, below).
      2. What to do with points but inability to negative double due to bad shape? Example: you have Axxx Qx Kxxx xx, partner opens 1♦ and RHO overcalls 1♥.
        • Bid 2♦ because you don't have tolerance for clubs. It is important to communicate your values.

  2. Opener's Rebid After a Negative Double

    1. Which suit?
      1. With four cards in either of the unbid suits, bid that suit
      2. Rebid own suit with a long strong suit
      3. Bid notrump with a stopper in the opponent's suit
      4. Choose between the unbid suits even with only three cards in the suit
    2. How high?
      1. Opener must, after the double, bid the full extent of his/her hand
      2. With a minimum (13-15), make a min bid
      3. With good hand (16-19 points), jump the bid
      4. With a maximum hand (20-21 points) bid game or cuebid opponent's suit.

  3. Reopening Double.

    1. When used?
      1. This is a tool of opener only. Never by responder or overcallers.
      2. It is set up when you open, LHO overcalls, partner passes and RHO passes.
      3. A double by you is now a reopening double.
      4. You strain to reopen on the auction above in order to protect partner's ability to punish the overcall.
      5. Exceptions:
        1. you are long in opponent's suit so partner cannot have a trump stack and must have passed out of weakness (ie: you hold 4+ cards)
        2. you are highly distributional - a one-suiter or you opened on the Rule of 20. This implies that your hand is not suitable for defense as you are short in high cards in the side suits.
        3. With 1 or 2 cards in enemy suit, strain to reopen with a double and then lead trump when in.
    2. When should responder leave in a reopening double?
      1. Leave the double in when you meet the 'Rule of 9' (If tricks bid by opponent + #cards in their suit + #honors in their suit add to 9 or more).
      2. Opener should strive to lead trump if possible when on lead.
      3. Otherwise clarify your hand with another bid, which opener will read as weak.
