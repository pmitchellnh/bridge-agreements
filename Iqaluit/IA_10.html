Direct Overcalls

  1. Simple (non-jump) Suit Overcalls

    1. One-Level. An overcall of 1♦, 1♥, or 1♠ promises:
      1. At least a 5-card suit that you would not be ashamed to have partner lead if we wind up on defense.
        • Overcall with a suit like Axxxx, KQxxx, KJTxx, QJ9xx
        • Do NOT overcall with Kxxxx, Q9xxx, Jxxxxx. Save these hands for possible balancing later
        • Balancing does NOT require a good suit and is NOT lead-directing
      2. 7-17 HCP
        • If only 7, then ALL, or nearly all, the HCP should be in the overcalling suit
        • AKxxx or AQJxx or KQJTx with an outside jack
    2. Two-Level. An overcall at the 2-level promises:
      1. A suit like that discussed in A1a, above.
      2. 9-17 HCP

  2. Responding to Partner's Simple Overcall

    1. If RHO passes
      1. Responses with a fit
        1. Less than 6 SP, pass
        2. 6-10 SP, raise partner.
        3. 11+ SP with 4+ trump support, cue bid opponent's suit.
          • Overcaller should repeat suit as cheaply as possible with minimum overcall
          • Overcaller should jump in suit or bid a second suit with a max overcall
        4. 16+ SP, bid game
        5. 5-card support (major) or 6-card support (minor) with a singleton or void elsewhere, bid game.
        6. 4-card support, weak and distributional, jump raise, preemptively
      2. Responses without a fit and with a balanced hand and with a stopper in opponent's suit
        1. 10-12 HCP, bid NT as cheaply as possible.
        2. 12-14 HCP, jump to 2NT from 1-level or just bid 2NT if already at 2-level.
        3. 15+ HCP, bid 3NT
      3. Responses without a fit and with 8+ HCP
        1. Strive to find a bid as partner may have 17 and a game is on.
        2. Note: a jump shift would be a weak jump shift!
      4. Otherwise Pass.
    2. If RHO bids, responses same as above except:
      1. 11+ SP with a fit:
        1. with 3+ trump support, cuebid opponents' first (opened) suit
        2. with 4+ trump support, cuebid opponents' second (responded) suit
      2. References to opponents' suit are now suit(s)
