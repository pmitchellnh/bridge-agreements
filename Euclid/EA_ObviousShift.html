Obvious Shift

  1. Defined

    • In response to partner's opening lead, any card played by third hand that is not an attempt to win the trick should be considered an obvious shift signal.
    • By this, we mean that a normally discouraging signal (high card) shows tolerance for the obvious shift suit. It does not necessarily deny support for the opened suit.
    • An encouraging signal denies tolerance for the obvious shift suit, and does not promise support for the opened suit.

  2. Determining the Obvious Shift Suit

    3. The obvious shift suit is determined by applying the following rules, in order, until a single suit is indicated:
    1. Eliminate the suit led.
    2. Eliminate the trump suit.
    3. Eliminate any suit in dummy headed by AKQ or KQJT or better.
    4. Against a suit contract, eliminate any suit in dummy that is singleton or void.
    5. Eliminate any suit bid naturally by declarer.
    6. Choose the suit(s) that has been bid naturally by you or partner. If none, continue; if multiple, eliminate non-bid suits and continue
    7. Suit only: Choose the three-card suit(s) in dummy headed by, at best, one honor
    8. Choose the remaining suit(s) in which dummy is shortest (even if it's AK doubleton). If multiple, eliminate longer suits and continue
    9. Choose the remaining suit(s) in which dummy has fewest honors.
    10. Choose the lower-ranked of the remaining suits.

  3. Tolerance for the Obvious Shift Means:

    4. If dummy's holding is: Then tolerance implies at least:
    Axx Qxx
    Kxx Qxx
    Qxx Kxx
    Jxx or worse (suit) Kxx
    Jxx or worse (NT) Qxx

    Note that tolerance of the obvious shift does not obligate opener to make such a shift. All things must be evaluated in context.
