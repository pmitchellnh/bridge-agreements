Smolen

  1. Defined

    1. Smolen is a transfer method used with 5/4 and 6/4 major suit hands in response to a 1NT opener when opener has no 4-card major.
    2. Partner has opened 1NT.
    3. Responder, with 5/4 or 6/4 in the majors and game-going values, bids 2♣ (Stayman)
    4. Opener responds 2♦ (no 4-card major)
      • 1NT - 2♣, 2♦ - ?
    5. Allows the 1NT opener to declare in a 5-3 or 6-2 fit, if found.

  2. Sequence

    1. Responder jumps in the 4-card suit!
      1. 1NT-2♣, 2♦-3♠!
        1. Tells opener that you have 4 spades and (at least) 5 hearts.
        2. Opener bids 4♥ with 3 hearts.
        3. Opener bids 3NT if holding a heart doubleton
          • With 6 hearts, responder persists with 4♦!
          • Opener bids 4♥ and plays in a 6-2 fit
      2. 1NT-2♣, 2♦-3♥!
        1. Tells opener that you have 5 spades and 4 hearts.
        2. Opener bids 4♠ with 3 spades.
        3. Opener bids 3NT if holding a spade doubleton
          • With 6 spades, responder persists with 4♥!
          • Opener bids 4♠ and plays in a 6-2 fit
