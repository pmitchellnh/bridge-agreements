Four Suit Transfers

  1. Transfer to clubs or diamonds with

    1. a 6-card minor bust
    2. a 6-cd hand invitational to 3NT
      • as weak as xx xxx KQxxxx xx will make 3NT if opener has Axx and stoppers elsewhere
    3. a 6-cd hand with slam interest
    4. a 6-cd hand with legitimate doubt as to whether 3NT or 5m is a better game
    5. use the diamond transfer with a weak 5/5 minors hand (pass any response)

  2. Transfer sequences

    1. The first transfer
      1. 2♦!! and 2♥!! are Jacoby transfers as usual.
      2. 2♠!! is a transfer to 3♣
        1. 3♣! postaccept
          • postaccept if you want to play 3NT should responder have the invitational hand (hand type 2 in the opening paragraph)
          • typically Qxx or xxxx or better
        2. else bid 2NT!
      3. 2N! is a transfer to 3♦
        1. 3♦! postaccept
        2. else bid 3♣!
    2. Responder's second bid after a postaccept
      1. Pass is to play (bust hand)
      2. 3NT is to play and shows you had the invitational hand.
      3. New suit! shows shortness.
        • Example: AKx xxx x AKxxxx, partner opens 1NT and you bid 2♠!
        • Partner transfers to 3♣
        • You bid 3♦! showing the diamond shortness.
        • Now 3NT by opener is to play and any other new suit is a cue.
        • Note that slam or 3NT or 5♣ may still be the final contract in the example hand (you'd really like to hear 3♥).
    3. Responder's second bid after a NON-postaccept
      1. Pass of 3♣ is to play (bust hand with 5-5 minors)
      2. Complete the transfer! with a bust or invitational hand (A or B, above)
      3. 3NT is a strong balanced slam try
      4. New suit! shows shortness (hand C or D, above)

  3. Stayman Subtleties

    1. Some minor adjustments are necessary since 1NT-2NT is not available any more or a simple invitation.
    2. Such hands now go thru Stayman:
      1. 1NT - 2♣, 2♦ - 2NT! might have 4-card major
      2. 1NT - 2♣, 2♠ - 2NT! might have 4 hearts
      3. 1NT - 2♣, 2♥ - 2NT! denies a 4-card major
      4. 1NT - 2♣, 2♥ - 2♠! shows 4 spades, 8-9 HCP
