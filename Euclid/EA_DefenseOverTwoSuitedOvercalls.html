Defense Over Two Suited Overcalls

  1. Defense vs Their Unusual NT

    1. On ONLY when we open a Major:
      • 1M (2NT) ?
    2. Double! shows a hand willing to penalize at least one of their suits
    3. 3♣! good hand in O
      1. Opener bids 2O with 3-card support
      2. Else opener bids 2M with 6-card suit
      3. Else opener bids 2♦ (temporizing)
    4. 3♦! limit or limit+ in M
    5. 3♥! competitive.
      1. if hearts opened, then we would have bid 2♥ without the interference
      2. if spades opened, then it suggests a weakish long heart suit without spade support
    6. 3♠! competitive.
      1. if spades opened, then we would have bid 2♠ without the interference
      2. if hearts opened, then it suggests a weakish long spade suit without heart support

  2. Defense Vs Their Michaels

    1. On ONLY when we open a Major
      • 1M (2M) ?
    2. Cuebid of their known (Major) suit is limit or limit+ for our opened major.
      1. Opener's rebid shows whether he would have accepted a limit raise.
        • 1♥ (2♥) 2♠! (p), 3♥ shows partner would NOT have accepted a limit raise
        • 1♠ (2♠) 3♥! (p), 3♠ show partner would NOT have accepted a limit raise
      2. This is another 'invisible cue bid'.
      3. Karen Walker cautions to be conservative here as suits will break badly. This means 3-card support is tenuous.
    3. Jump cuebids! or 4m! are strong splinters and are slam tries in our opened major.
    4. Double! or NT shows all non-bigfit hands with 10+ HCP.
    5. All other bids are non-forcing
      1. Competitive raises of our opened major
      2. Free bids of new suits
