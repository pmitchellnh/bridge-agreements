Balancing

  1. Balancing Overcalls Over One of a Suit

    1. Pass when weak (0-7 HCP) OR if long in the opponent's suit and it is your best suit.
    2. Double when holding a strong NT (15+) OR any 8+ HCP short in the opponent's suit (partner may pass to convert to a penalty double)
      1. Partner's response
        1. Pass for penalty (must be 12+ with 5 trumps)
        2. Non-jump bid 0-11 HCP
        3. 2NT is 12-14 balanced, with at least 2 stoppers in opponent's suit.
        4. Jump in a suit shows 12+ HCP
        5. Cuebid shows 12+ HCP but no good suit and unwilling to bid NT.
      2. Rebids by doubler
        1. Pass a suit with 8-11 HCP
        2. Bid or raise with 12+ HCP
        3. Bid NT with the strong NT holding
    3. Overcall in a suit with 7+ HCP without shortness in the opponent's suit and with a bad suit somewhere.

  2. Balancing Over an Opponent's Fit at the Two Level

    1. We must balance (bid or double) at matchpoints unless all three of the following are true:
      1. we are vulnerable (risk factor - going for minus 200).
      2. we have more than a singleton in the opponent's suit.
        • Increasing numbers of cards in their suit reduce our fit probability.
        • Shortness in their suit increases it.
      3. no 2-level balance is even theoretically possible.
        • A 3-level bid increases the chance of a penalty double.
    2. Examples of opponents showing a fit:
      • (1♥) p (2♥) p, (p) ?
      • (1♦) p (1♥) p, (2♥) p (p) ?
